Include one example of something the Peer Coach could say and explain how this supports long-term skill development instead of short-term completion.

One approach: the Peer Coach names the pattern rather than the person, keeps the tone curious instead of corrective, and redirects the student toward the learning goal behind the assignment.

"It sounds like the deadline is the real pressure here. Want to try asking AI to explain the concept back to you first, so you're still the one doing the writing? That way you walk into the exam actually knowing the material."

Why it supports long-term development: this framing doesn't shame the student for considering a shortcut — it acknowledges the real pressure (time, stress, confusion) while pointing back to what the assignment is actually for: building the skill, not just producing the artifact. A student who is talked through this once is more likely to make the same judgment call independently next time, which is the actual goal of coaching.
